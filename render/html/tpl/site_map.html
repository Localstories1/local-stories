  • This site (philosophy) : happenstance and serendipity applied to information

    • There is more truth in what we do not know in what we hope to know.
      • Information has properties that make its use unpredictable.
      • Designing an IS is especially organizing adaptation , openness and the "good" risk are needed.
      • New digital tools radically change the situation ( eg indexing instead of databases engines, BI webservices ... )
      • These tools will be operating with a methodology adapted exit: ITIL , Lean , PMP ...
      • A systemic vision : first I see the limits of what I know and associated experiences and I deduced where I need to be more curious or critical to understand the meaning of the overall system .
      • I am more interested in what I do not know that I know . I did not certainty but I find opportunities and reconciliations galore.
      • This is a fairly empirical and opposed to scientific approaches and forecasters who depart from the existing experience and finished and specific context to associate recognized that makes the most consensus solution approach.
      • Traditional approaches successfully solve 85% of problems.
      • With a different way of thinking , I'm more efficient, in anticipation, the strongs problems that resist the hardest and are the critical issues for the development of the company.
    • A company is not producing but sharing experences high value for maximum direct and indirect stakeholders
      • The triptych "Cost quality time" is bullshit, the ROI is leading the relationship should last, projects must allow great experiences
      • Skills are less important than people and their history and thier vision (about past and futur)
      • Support activities (financial, accounting, purchasing, human resources, overhead, secretarial ...) disturb the reality of business. These are activities that generate sustained non-performing positions. But every employee should have the opportunity to directly participate in most important: the creation, production and sharing with the client.
